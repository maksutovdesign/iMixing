# iMixing Marketing Playbook

Updated: 2026-05-30

## 1. Core Positioning

### Main Product Stories

`Mix & Master`

- Bad track in -> finished release-ready master out.
- User uploads stems, gets a cleaner mix, a mastered WAV, and a visible before/after result.
- Best angle for ads: "From messy session to finished track in minutes."

`MIDI Doctor`

- Bad MIDI in -> cleaner, more musical MIDI out.
- User uploads one `.mid` file, picks a style, changes settings, downloads a repaired version.
- Best angle for ads: "Fix weak MIDI ideas before opening the DAW for hours."

### Short Value Propositions

For Russia:

- "Загрузил дорожки, получил более собранный микс и master."
- "Исправь MIDI за минуту вместо ручной правки нот."
- "Понять, что не так со звуком, и быстро получить результат."

For global:

- "Upload stems, get a cleaner mix and ready master."
- "Repair messy MIDI before losing hours in the piano roll."
- "Fast AI cleanup for demos, sketches, and release prep."

## 2. Audience Segments

### Primary

- Bedroom producers who do not finish tracks.
- Beatmakers who need fast demo polish.
- Songwriters with rough sessions and separate stems.
- Producers working with MIDI drafts from packs, sketches, or collaborators.

### Secondary

- Vocalists recording over bought beats.
- Small studios needing fast pre-mix or demo mastering.
- Music schools, course creators, and producer communities.

## 3. Best Go-To-Market Angles

### Angle A: Evolution Of Sound

Use for `Mix & Master`.

- Start with pain: muddy, unbalanced, clipped, amateur.
- Show action: upload stems.
- Show result: cleaner balance, louder master, visible waveform improvement.
- CTA: try one demo job, compare before/after.

### Angle B: MIDI Rescue

Use for `MIDI Doctor`.

- Start with pain: crowded piano roll, weak voicing, random velocities.
- Show action: choose style or settings.
- Show result: cleaner notes, stronger harmony, easier DAW workflow.
- CTA: upload one file and hear the difference.

## 4. Region Strategy

### Russia

Priority channels:

- VK for paid reach and retargeting.
- Telegram for channel-native ads and founder content.
- Yandex Search for intent traffic.
- VC.ru, Habr-like adjacent communities, DTF-style creator spaces where case-study content performs.
- YouTube Shorts, VK Clips, Telegram video posts for organic proof.

What to emphasize:

- Quick result without a full studio workflow.
- Russian-language interface and support.
- Practical use cases: demo, beat, vocal draft, arrangement cleanup.
- Payments and onboarding adapted for Russia when payment stack is ready.

### Global

Priority channels:

- Google Ads for high-intent demand capture.
- TikTok for creator-facing short video distribution.
- Reddit for community-native targeting around music production.
- Product Hunt for launch visibility.
- X, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels for organic founder-led demos.
- Discord and Reddit communities for manual seeding and feedback loops.

What to emphasize:

- AI workflow speed.
- Before/after proof.
- "No need to clean the piano roll manually."
- "Useful for demos, client drafts, and idea validation."

## 5. Free Promotion Plan

### Highest ROI Organic Moves

1. Publish short before/after videos 3 to 5 times per week.
2. Post "bad session -> fixed result" carousels.
3. Publish teardown threads: what was wrong, what the service changed, what the user got.
4. Build a small library of free examples:
   one bad MIDI file, one fixed MIDI file, one rough stem pack, one finished master.
5. Post founder notes in Telegram and X:
   tiny product updates, real demos, limitations, improvements.
6. Launch a small free community challenge:
   "send your worst MIDI" or "send 15-second stems."
7. Turn every product update into 4 formats:
   short video, square post, carousel, article.

### Organic Channel Priority

Russia:

- Telegram channel
- VK community
- YouTube Shorts
- VC.ru articles
- профильные Telegram-чаты по музыке и продакшену

Global:

- YouTube Shorts
- TikTok
- Reddit posts in relevant subreddits
- Product Hunt launch and updates
- X threads
- Discord communities for producers

## 6. Paid Promotion Plan

### Budget Tier 1: Lean Test

Russia:

- 30,000 to 60,000 RUB per month.
- Split: 40% VK, 40% Yandex Search, 20% Telegram seed tests.

Global:

- $500 to $1,500 per month.
- Split: 45% Google Search, 25% TikTok, 20% Reddit, 10% retargeting.

Goal:

- Find which message converts:
  `upload stems`, `fix MIDI`, or `before/after`.

### Budget Tier 2: Working Growth

Russia:

- 80,000 to 200,000 RUB per month.
- Split: 35% VK, 35% Yandex, 20% Telegram Ads, 10% retargeting/lookalikes.

Global:

- $2,000 to $5,000 per month.
- Split: 35% Google Search, 25% TikTok, 20% Reddit, 10% YouTube video, 10% retargeting.

Goal:

- Scale the winning message by audience segment:
  beatmakers, songwriters, small studios, MIDI-first producers.

### Paid Creative Rules

- Use real before/after proof, not generic AI claims.
- Lead with one painful problem in the first second or first line.
- Show interface or transformation, not abstract branding only.
- Keep one message per ad:
  either `mix/master` or `MIDI repair`.

## 7. Funnel Structure

### Mix & Master Funnel

1. Ad or organic clip:
   "Your demo sounds flat because the stems fight each other."
2. Landing view:
   upload stems, compare rough mix vs master, understand what changed.
3. Offer:
   first free or low-friction demo.
4. Retargeting:
   show before/after example and social proof.

### MIDI Doctor Funnel

1. Ad or organic clip:
   "This MIDI took 30 seconds to clean."
2. Landing view:
   upload MIDI, choose style, download fixed file.
3. Offer:
   one free export or free credits.
4. Retargeting:
   show piano-roll cleanup and style options.

## 8. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Before / After

- rough track vs master
- messy MIDI vs repaired MIDI
- waveform, piano-roll, meter, or note-density comparisons

### Pillar 2: Education

- why tracks sound muddy
- why MIDI sounds fake
- how gain staging helps
- why voicing and velocity matter

### Pillar 3: Product Proof

- real UI
- real input/output examples
- fast turnaround demos
- transparent product limitations and improvements

### Pillar 4: Community

- user submissions
- challenges
- feedback reviews
- ranking worst MIDI mistakes or stem mistakes

## 12. Metrics To Track

Top of funnel:

- thumb-stop rate
- video hold rate at 3 seconds
- CTR
- saves and shares

Mid funnel:

- landing page conversion to upload
- first upload completion rate
- cost per upload

Bottom funnel:

- cost per activated user
- repeat usage within 7 days
- percent of users who try both workflows
